When a private equity firm or M&A advisor engages us for a technology assessment, they want clarity on a simple question: what is the real state of this company's technology, and what will it cost to get it where it needs to be?

Our assessment framework has been refined across hundreds of engagements. Here is how we approach it, step by step.

## Phase 1: Discovery and Inventory

Every assessment starts with a complete systems inventory. We catalog every piece of software, infrastructure component, and integration in the environment. This goes beyond what the target's IT team can typically produce from memory.

We examine four domains:

- **Core business applications.** ERP, CRM, financial systems, and any industry-specific platforms that run daily operations.
- **Infrastructure.** Cloud environments, on-premises servers, networking equipment, and hosting arrangements.
- **Data architecture.** Databases, data warehouses, ETL pipelines, and reporting tools. We map where data lives and how it moves.
- **Security posture.** Identity management, endpoint protection, backup and recovery systems, and compliance tooling.

The discovery phase typically takes three to five business days depending on the complexity of the environment.

## Phase 2: Architecture and Risk Analysis

With the inventory complete, we evaluate the architectural quality of the technology stack. This is where the real insight emerges.

We assess each system against five criteria:

**Scalability.** Can the current architecture support the growth assumptions in the deal model? If the business plan calls for 3x revenue growth over the hold period, will the technology stack handle that without a rearchitecture project?

**Maintainability.** Is the technology supported by the vendor? Are there available engineers who can work on it? We check end-of-life dates, community support, and labor market availability for every major platform.

**Integration readiness.** How easily can this system connect with an acquirer's existing portfolio or a bolt-on target's infrastructure? We evaluate API availability, data export capabilities, and standards compliance.

**Single points of failure.** We identify every dependency where one component, one vendor, or one employee represents an unacceptable concentration of risk.

**Compliance alignment.** Does the technology environment meet the regulatory requirements applicable to the business, including SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA, or industry-specific frameworks?

## Phase 3: Cost Modeling

This is where technology assessment translates into deal language. We build a detailed cost model that covers three scenarios:

1. **Maintain as-is.** The annual run-rate cost of keeping the current environment operational, including upcoming license renewals, required security patches, and end-of-life replacements.

2. **Optimize.** Targeted improvements that reduce risk and operational cost without a full transformation. This typically includes consolidating redundant systems, upgrading critical security gaps, and automating manual processes.

3. **Modernize.** A comprehensive roadmap to bring the technology stack to current standards, aligned with the value creation plan for the hold period.

Each scenario includes timeline estimates, resource requirements, and a risk-adjusted cost range.

## Phase 4: Executive Deliverable

The final output is not a technical document. It is a concise executive summary designed for deal teams and investment committees. We translate technical findings into financial impact, integration risk, and operational readiness metrics.
